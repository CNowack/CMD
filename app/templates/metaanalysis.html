SIG1/SIG2 Microbiome Signature Search — Prevalence Meta-analysis

The composition of the gut microbiome at treatment initiation has been shown to predict response to immune checkpoint blockade (ICB) across multiple cancer types. Derosa et al. (Cell, 2024) identified two mutually exclusive microbial communities in advanced NSCLC patients. SIG1, comprising 37 species associated with gut dysbiosis and poor immunotherapy response, and SIG2, comprising 45 species associated with a healthy gut ecosystem and favorable ICB outcomes. Patients dominated by SIG1 genera at baseline had significantly shorter overall survival on anti-PD-1/PD-L1 therapy independent of PD-L1 expression, antibiotic use, and performance status. The analysis below applies this framework to the BMC cancer microbiome cohort, asking whether the same immunotherapy-relevant genera are detected across cancer types beyond NSCLC.

This meta-analysis is purely exploratory and hypothesis generating. Because the BMC cohort used 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing and the Derosa cohort used shotgun metagenomics, abundance values are not directly comparable. Instead, genus-level presence/absence, shown here as prevalence, was utilized to explore these genera. Presence of a genus is defined as relative genus abundance > 0. The Derosa NSCLC cohort serves as a validated reference while the BMC cohort is hypothesis-generating.
