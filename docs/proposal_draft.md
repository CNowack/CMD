1. Brief Description of Project
Since 2019, buccal, stool, and nasal samples have been collected from 142 BMC patients with various cancer types at different timepoints over the course of their care at BMC, totaling 547 samples. As samples were collected over 5 years, 16S rRNA sequencing was performed on samples in 7 different batches. Compiled raw sequencing data was processed using ampliseq, a nextflow amplicon sequencing analysis workflow that utilizes DADA2 and QIIME2 to infer amplicon sequence variants (ASVs) from raw amplicon data. Abundance tables were generated, containing the abundance of each individual taxa across all samples, and QC of each sample data was obtained using fastqc. Sample data was then organized and aligned with patient metadata from BMC clinical records.

This project aims to design and implement a relational database that integrates the biological 16S rRNA microbiome sequencing data with clinical patient metadata from BMC records, consolidating key variables such as cancer type, immunotherapy drug, and treatment response status into a unified and queryable resource that accounts for sample site, collection timepoint, and sequencing batch. We aim to develop an interactive web application that provides researchers and clinicians with an accessible interface to explore microbiome composition through graphical analysis such as phylogenetic trees of abundant taxa and overall abundance. Lastly, we aim to incorporate longitudinal tracking functionality that allows users to visualize shifts in individual patient microbiome profiles across treatment timepoints and align them with clinical outcomes with the goal of identifying microbial signatures associated with immunotherapy response and disease progression. 2. Key Research Questions
Our database will answer the following broad questions:

Does the microbiome composition of cancer patients differ based on clinical characteristics (i.e., cancer type)?

Is microbiome composition associated with immunotherapy treatment response and cancer progression outcomes?

How does the microbiome change longitudinally over the course of cancer treatment?

3. User Accessibility
Our database is publicly available with no login required. This includes 16S rRNA sequencing data and categorical clinical variables such as cancer type that carry low re-identification risk when not linked to a specific individual.

4. Description of Data
Clinical Metadata: Originates from BMC medical records and will include unidentifiable patient features such as disease and treatment variables including cancer type and category, immunotherapy drug, combination treatment type, and response status.

Biological Sample Data: Originates from 547 samples collected from 142 BMC cancer patients across three anatomical sites (buccal, stool, and nasal) at multiple timepoints over the course of each patient's care at BMC since 2019. Samples were processed over 5 years across 7 sequencing batches using 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing.

Microbiome Sequencing Data: Generated from raw fastq 16S rRNA sequencing files that were processed through the ampliseq nextflow pipeline, which utilizes DADA2 for amplicon sequence variant (ASV) inference and QIIME2 for taxonomy assignment. These results included:

ASV abundance tables containing the raw counts and relative abundances of each taxon across all samples.

Taxonomy assignment tables mapping each ASV to its full taxonomic hierarchy from phylum to species.

Representative ASV sequences.

Per-sample quality control metrics generated by FastQC including read depth and quality scores.
